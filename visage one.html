Harmonizing Jazz and Barber house

Hong Kong's Visage One Live Hub

Content Curator

Nestled within the vibrant urban landscape of Hong Kong lies Visage One, a unique establishment that seamlessly blends the art of barber house with the soulful melodies of jazz music. Founded 21 years ago by Benky, a professional barber with a deep passion for jazz, Visage One has become a beloved destination for music enthusiasts every Saturday evening.

"I'll wrap up the haircuts equipment around 5:30 PM. Then, I set up the tables and chairs needed for the evening. I like to dim the lights and add some candlelight to the space," explains Benky, "Around 8 PM, the musicians arrive and set up their instruments for the performance."

He looks forward to the audience's arrival during the conversation, eagerly immersing himself in the evening's musical feast.

This modest barbershop of just 33 square meters, after a week of bright days, transforms into a live jazz scene on Saturday nights. Candles flicker on tables, chairs of varying heights are arranged, creating a symphony of sight with various musical instruments. The familiar barbershop atmosphere evolves into a vibrant jazz venue, inviting musicians from Hong Kong and beyond to showcase their talent.

It was a special night yesterday, marking the first collaborative performance at Visage One by Scott and Eric. Scott, a full-time saxophonist who recently released a new album, adding to his repertoire of musical works, had performed at this venue four years ago with another musician who has since moved to London. Eric, a Hong Kong guitarist and a regular in the local music scene, has also showcased his talent in Europe and mainland China. Meanwhile, Eric has known Benky, the barbershop owner, for nearly eight years. Their longstanding connection with Visage One highlights the longevity of a vibrant music community.

"I composed this piece specifically for this occasion, with the saxophone as the base. It's fun because it conveys the concept of harmonious coexistence between people or entities, sharing similar feelings and emotions," Scott introduced one of the pieces they played, "The concept of cooperation and mutual understanding plays a crucial role in jazz. Hence, I named this piece 'Simpatico,' hoping to showcase the harmonious collaboration between me and Eric."

In fact, at the start of the performance, the resonance between the saxophone and the guitar was not harmonious. The deep echoes of the sax clashed with the guitar's light melodies. After an hour of adjustment, the conflict between the deep saxophone echoes and the light guitar melodies slowly evolved into a harmonious blend, like skilled craftsmen polishing their art. They deftly adjusted their sounds until both melodies perfectly intertwined.

"In that hour," remarked Yiman, an audience member, during the exchange, "you could clearly see the transformation of your collaboration from the initial discord to a beautiful harmony." She was deeply captivated by the performance and believed it was a perfect display of the charm of jazz and the essence of Visage One. This inclusive and vibrant place enchants every visitor with its irresistible allure.

"That's great," Scott replied, "That’s exactly what we want."

On a warm early summer night in Hong Kong, jazz music slowly unfolds in a cozy atmosphere. Wall-mounted electric fans bring a gentle breeze, their soft hum mingling with the rustle of audience attire and the creaking of wooden chairs, blending inadvertently with the band's melody. Everyone is immersed in this vibrant night, breathing in unison with the jazz, feeling every beat of the notes.

The retro interior of the building is cleverly designed. A narrow staircase without handrails leading up to a two-meter-wide L-shaped loft.

On the first floor, the audience squeezes into chairs of various heights and styles, arranged in a chaotic yet dynamic manner. Some sit on the edge of the stairs, focused on the performance unfolding below. Even those standing by the entrance bar are immersed, becoming part of defining the vivid scenes and sounds of Visage One.

On the second floor, others sit on the back row of crates or chairs, or simply on the floor, looking down at the performance through the iron railing.

The weekly brief and intimate audio-visual feast is all thanks to Benky. With twenty-one years of Visage One history, musicians often reach out to him asking to perform. While in Hong Kong, he doesn't use social media, relying only on the company phone to connect with musicians. Even those from London or New York get in touch by phone calls.
