In the heart of downtown Manhattan at One Hanover Square, surrounded by towering skyscrapers, 80% of workers in this area report feeling stressed on the job. Financial district employees typically work 13-14 hours a day on average, with an additional 43-minute daily commute into the city. This demanding schedule leaves them exhausted and overwhelmed.


In bustling urban environments, numerous distractions hinder individuals from obtaining adequate rest. This sanctuary aims to mitigate or eliminate these distractions, enabling people to rejuvenate and recharge effectively.

“Our physical bodies have been neglected throughout the fundamental nature of our existence. Ursula Franklin attributes this trend to the application of prescriptive technology in our daily lives and processes. This has created a ‘marked decrease in the reliance of people on their own experience and their own senses.’”
--Bohme, Gernot. “Atmosphere as Mindful Physical Presence in Space”

What is distraction?
There are two main forms of distractions, internal distractions which come from an individuals’ mind and external distractions which come from the surrounding environments. Internal and external distractions limit sufficient rest in busy and chaotic city.
Individuals receive all the information through sense. In order to redirect, weaken or eliminate these distractions, this sanctuary propose to use spatial experiences to categorize, prioritize and redirect all the sensory information including touching, hearing, vision, taste, smell through sensory substitution.


Design Strategy-Sensory Substitution

Changes the characteristics of one sensory modality into stimuli of another sensory modality to categorize, prioritize and redirect all the sensory information


Intersection: The intersection will be explored through intersected programs to redirect distractions.

Adjacency: The adjacency will be translated through a semi-open space to weaken distraction.

Boundary: The boundary will filter redundant sensory information in the space.

The following programs respond to the need for rapid decompression in a bustling urban environment, offering a sensory meditative experience to help alleviate stress and fatigue. The amenities include a pool, sauna rooms, steam room, meditation spaces, floating rooms, napping rooms, massage rooms, a retail area, and a semi-buffet restaurant. Each experience is centered around sensory information for optimal relaxation.

Entering

Individuals are entering the Es-sense-tial Ab-sense through a tiny entrance which is hidden under a restaurant on the Pearl street. It leads individuals to go all the way down to the MINUS TWO level.
When they are going down through the stair, their auditory sense substitute the visual sense to dominent the process of perceiving Street sensory information. The strategy adjacency is apply. This is the back street of many restaurant. The contrast between the chaotic noise on the street and the quitness inside the building is strong.

In this dimly lit environment with the soothing sound of water, a deliberate boundary strategy is at play. The darkness acts as a visual barrier, prompting the auditory sense to take the lead in perception. This transition occurs as visitors descend the stairs from the bustling street above, marking the juncture I previously highlighted in discussing the adjacency strategy between the lively street and this tranquil oasis.

Washing up- Enter Resting State

Strategy: Adjacency, Boundary
This juncture, the visual and auditory senses enhance the tactile experience within this intimate meditation chamber. Employing a boundary strategy, it features a raised wall and a water bed that conveys the soothing vibrations of water, drawing attention away from external distractions and towards the serene sounds of water for a tranquil meditation experience. The concept of the water bed is inspired by the meditation posture known as Corpse Pose (Savasana).

The whole space is presented with the lowest illumination, and the fog produced by the water surface weakens people's vision to a certain extent. At this time, their sense of touch and hearing become the dominant sense. at the interstection point, the pool will borrow light from the shallow water pathway




Strategy: Boundary
The sauna seamlessly integrates into the pool, allowing pool users to experience a unique and relaxing sauna session as they pass by. Sound Substitute the visual sense to help individuals navigate themselves at some points.

Resting

Strategy: Intersection, Adjacency
The upper floor consists of napping beds, while the lower floor features a 90-minute cycle semi-self-service restaurant with a flowing bar counter. The floor and tabletop of the bar area downstairs are connected to the net supporting the napping beds above through vertical steel cables, creating a resonant structure. Partially open boundaries allow food aromas to permeate through. When the upper area transitions into a resting state, the lower area follows suit. As the 90-minute rest period approaches, servers carrying tantalizing food aromas enter the area with gentle footsteps to set up the service counter. Vibration is created when people step on the steel panel or place items on the table, as both are under one frame connected to the sleeping net above. People are gently awakened from their slumber by the aroma of food and the gentle bed vibrations, rather than by annoying phone alarms or loud morning calls.

Eating: Absorbing Energy, Reconnecting to Outside World

Strategy: Boundary
Due to the window height and seat-to-wall distance, when seated, the street view is obscured, offering a vista limited to Hanover Square alone. Enclosed by sound-reflecting metal, the space resonates with external noise, grounding individuals in the present moment. The street sounds serve as a reminder of reality, blending with the ambient environment.

This is a point of readjusting the boundaries between the sanctuary and the external world.
